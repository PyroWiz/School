Company Policy Handbook
1. Vacation & Time Off Policies

Annual Vacation Days: Full-time employees receive 22 paid vacation days per calendar year.

Seniority Bonus Days: After 3 years, employees receive 2 additional vacation days annually, up to a maximum of 28 total days per year.

Request Procedure: Vacation requests must be submitted through the HR portal at least 10 business days in advance.

Blackout Periods: Due to operational demands, vacation during March 20–April 10 and October 1–October 20 requires explicit managerial approval.

Carryover Limit: Employees may carry over up to 8 unused vacation days to the following year.

Sick Leave: Employees are entitled to 12 paid sick days annually. Absences exceeding 2 consecutive days require a medical certificate.

Personal Days: Employees may use 3 personal days per year for urgent or unforeseen matters.

Family Leave: Up to 5 paid days for immediate family emergencies or medical needs.

Unpaid Leave: May be granted at management’s discretion for up to 60 days, depending on business needs and employee circumstances.

2. Dress Code Standards

General Expectations: All employees must maintain a professional, neat, and respectful appearance.

Business Casual (Mon–Thu):

Acceptable: slacks, skirts, dresses, blouses, collared shirts, sweaters, clean dress shoes.

Accessories should remain modest and appropriate for a corporate environment.

Casual Fridays:

Jeans, sneakers, and company-branded shirts are permitted.

Clothing must remain clean, intact, and non-offensive.

Prohibited Attire:

Ripped clothing, flip-flops, gym wear, beachwear, overly revealing outfits, clothing with offensive graphics or language.

Client-Facing Days: Employees meeting clients must wear business formal attire (blazer, dress shoes, tailored garments).

3. Travel & Transportation Reimbursement

Public Transportation: Up to 350₪ per month reimbursed for bus, train, or light rail passes with valid receipts.

Personal Vehicle Use: Reimbursed at 1.4₪ per kilometer for approved business-related travel (excluding daily commute).

Taxi Use: Allowed with prior managerial approval or after 22:00 for employee safety.

Parking Fees: Reimbursed only when traveling to client sites.

Car Rentals: Permitted for out-of-town business travel when approved in advance.

Travel Advances: Employees may request a travel advance for multi-day business trips.

Submission Deadline: Expense reports must be submitted within 30 days of the expense date.

4. Work Hours, Flexibility & Remote Work

Standard Office Hours: 09:00–18:00 with a 1-hour lunch break.

Flexible Hours: Employees may begin work anytime between 07:00 and 10:00, pending managerial approval.

Remote Work:

Up to 2 remote workdays per week for eligible employees.

Employees must maintain reliable internet and a work-appropriate setting.

Overtime: Must be pre-approved. Compensation or time-off-in-lieu provided based on company policy and local laws.

Breaks: Employees are encouraged to take short breaks to promote health, productivity, and well-being.

5. Employee Conduct & Professional Standards

Respectful Communication: Employees must communicate respectfully with colleagues, partners, and clients.

Ethics & Integrity: Honesty, transparency, and professionalism are expected at all times.

Confidentiality: All sensitive information must remain internal and may not be disclosed without authorization.

Conflict of Interest: Employees must declare any external employment, freelance work, or business activity that could conflict with company interests.

Anti-Harassment Policy: Zero tolerance for harassment, discrimination, bullying, or any form of threatening behavior.

Workplace Behavior: Employees must maintain a safe, inclusive, and collaborative work environment.

6. Technology, Data Protection & Cybersecurity

Device Usage: Company laptops and mobile devices are for work purposes; minimal personal use is tolerated.

Password Policy: Passwords must be updated every 90 days, include 12+ characters, and meet complexity standards.

Data Protection: Sensitive data may only be stored in approved systems (cloud tools, internal servers). External drives require IT approval.

Software Installation: Only IT-approved software may be installed on company devices.

Network Security: Employees must use VPN when accessing internal systems remotely.

Phishing Awareness: Employees must complete annual cybersecurity training and report suspicious emails immediately.

7. Office Environment, Facilities & Workplace Etiquette

Workspace Cleanliness: Employees must keep their desks tidy and maintain a clutter-free environment.

Meeting Rooms: Must be booked in advance and left clean and reset for the next group.

Kitchen Policy:

Employees must wash dishes immediately after use.

Food left in the refrigerator for over 72 hours may be discarded.

Common Areas: Respect shared spaces and maintain noise levels appropriate for a professional environment.

Headphones Requirement: All audio—calls, music, videos—must be played through headphones.

Safety & Emergency Procedures: All employees must participate in annual safety drills and follow evacuation guidelines.

8. Employee Benefits & Development

Training Budget: Annual 2,000₪ allowance for courses, workshops, certifications, or conferences.

Wellness Program: Monthly 100₪ subsidy for gym memberships or sports activities.

Team Events: Quarterly team-building events and an annual company retreat.

Health Insurance Upgrades: Optional supplementary health packages at discounted corporate rates.

Employee Recognition Program: Quarterly awards for outstanding performance, innovation, and teamwork.

Mentorship Program: New hires can request a mentor for professional integration and growth.

9. Performance Management

Annual Performance Review: Conducted once per year, including feedback, goal setting, and career planning.

Mid-Year Check-In: Optional evaluation to ensure progress toward objectives.

Promotion Pathways: Transparent criteria for advancement, based on performance, skills, and contribution.

Corrective Actions: If performance concerns arise, employees may enter an Improvement Plan with managerial support.

10. Company Assets & Resource Management

Equipment Responsibility: Employees are responsible for maintaining company equipment in proper working condition.

Return of Equipment: All devices and access cards must be returned upon termination or role change.

Purchasing Requests: Office equipment or software requests must be approved by the direct manager and procurement team.
